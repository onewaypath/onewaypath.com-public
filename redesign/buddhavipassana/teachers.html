About

The Buddhavipassana Meditation Centre is operated by One Way Path Communities in mid-town, Toronto, Canada. We provide insight meditation based on the four foundations of mindfulness in the Therevada Buddhist Tradition.

Insight Meditation Courses

Resident Teachers

The Buddhavipassana Meditation Centre offers extensive, in depth and authentic training in the Buddhist method of spiritual growth and development known as insight meditation. These courses also serve as an introduction to Buddhism as practiced in the northern Thai tradition. Courses are by donation and we request a minimum 15-day commitment from meditators who are new to this style of practice.

Resident
Teachers

Miriam Young

Miriam Young completed her basic course in Insight Meditation in 2002 with Edward Kooij in Hamilton, Canada and continued practicing with Edward as her teacher. She was later temporarily ordained as a Buddhist Nun (Maechee) in the Theravada tradition by Ajahn Tong Sirimangalo from 2002 until 2004. During this time she began the meditation teacher training under Ajahns Thanat and Kathryn Chindaporn and later also trained with Sandra Luz Cibrian.

Miriam was appointed as a Vipassana Meditation Teacher by Most Venerable Ajahn Tong Sirimangalo in 2013. She has received the Benefactor of Buddhism Award of Chiangmai Province (2014) and the Sema Dhammacakka in Recognition of International Dissemination of Buddhism (2018), the latter conferred by HRH Princess Maha Chakri Sirindhorn of Thailand.

Alex Young

Alex Young first began practicing Insight Meditation in Hamilton, Canada in 2001 and continued practicing with Edward Kooij as his first teacher. He began teacher training under the guidance of Ajahns Thanat and Kathryn Chindaporn in 2006 and later also with Sandra Luz Cibrian.

Alex was appointed as a Vipassana Meditation Teacher by Most Venerable Ajahn Tong Sirimangalo in 2013. He has received the Benefactor of Buddhism Award of Chiangmai Province (2014) and the Sema Dhammacakka in Recognition of International Dissemination of Buddhism (2020), the latter conferred by HRH Princess Maha Chakri Sirindhorn of Thailand.

Sandra Young

Sandra Young completed her basic course in Insight Meditation in 2002 with Edward Kooij in Hamilton, Canada and continued practicing with Edward for several years. In 2016, Sandra travelled to Chomtong, Thailand where she remained resident for over a year in order to undertake the teacher training under Ajahns Thanat and Kathryn Chindaporn.

Sandra has received the Benefactor of Buddhism Award of Chiangmai Province (2017) and was appointed as a Vipassana Meditation Teacher by Venerable Ajahn Tong Sirimangalo in 2018
