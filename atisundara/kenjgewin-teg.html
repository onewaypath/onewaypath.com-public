About

The Atisundara Foundation provides funding for peacebuilding projects to promote reconciliaton and healing from past conflict. Activities funded by the foundation include insight meditation in the Therevada Buddhist Tradition and the protection and preservation of endangered languages and cultures.

    • Kenjgwin Teg Early Childhood Education Bursary

    The Atisundara Foundation funds a bursary for learners enrolled in an early childhood education program at Kenjgewin Teg, a government recognized education institution owned and operated independently by several First Nation communities approximately 400 km north of Toronto. Kenjgewin Teg supports the unique needs of Indigenous learners with an emphasis on strengthening the local language and culture which is lived by the Anishinaabe People of the Mnidoo Mnising (Manitoulin Island).

    Program Description

    In 2022, Kenjgewin Teg used our funding to provide a $500/student bursary to 4 of the 13 students in its Early Childhood Education Program. In order to select the bursary recipients, Kenjgewin Teg decided to create an online form using the standard features of Microsoft Office365.

    Applicants were asked to provide biographical information along with a submission addressing three areas identified in the Bursary Application Form:

    1. Prior participation with Kenjgewin Tegâ€™s cultural program called Anishinaabe Aadziwin
    2. Academic participation to date
    3. Personal growth reflection

    Applicants were permitted to make their submission in written or other forms such as videos or other types of artwork. We were delighted to learn that at least one applicant made a submission in his native language.

    The bursary was undersubscribed by the initial applicants, so Kenjgewin Teg prompted enough applicants to use all the funding. Prior to making the award, Kenjgewin Teg explicitly informed all of the recipients that each bursary award is a one-time award and that there is no guarantee of a bursary for subsequent years. For 2023, the Foundation aims to increase the size of the program to $4,000 per year.
